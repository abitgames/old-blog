Designing Enemy Spawn: Part 1

Written on February 16, 2011 by nantas in Blog

I’ve been recently working on setting up enemy spawn in our top-down shooter game. This is an old topic for level design, usually your goals would be:

  1. Keep bringing dudes to the player, so he can keep killing ‘em
  2. Spawn dudes out of player’s sight
  3. Keep AI count under budget

Sounds easy, huh? Let’s see a simple example to achieve this goal.

Spawn Type 1: Trigger and Respawn

In this top view doodle, the little figure at the bottom represent player. Two triangles are two AI spawner hide behind solid objects. When player reaches the shaded area, it triggers the two spawners and start spawn AIs.

Let’s assume two spawner each spawns one AI and our AI count budget is 2. So our second and third goal are achieved. But we are not sure about the first goal yet. What if player just stay in this area after he kills that 2 dudes? Having nothing to do is not fun. How about respawn AIs from spawners as soon as the spawned AIs are killed?

Usually I will respawn AIs once or twice before player gets bored with this area. It’s a solid solution to give player some time to learn the geometry and art of the area, enjoy the combat and eventually free to move on.

But that’s not a perfect solution, imagine player triggers AI spawn, and move forward until he can see spawners directly, then kills the AIs. Oops, now our goal #2 is ruined.

Here’s what we need to fix the problem:

Spawn Type 1a: Trigger to Disable Respawn

The new shaded rectangle is another trigger to disable the respawn behavior of the two spawner. As soon as player reach the position where he can see the spawners, it makes sure those spawners stop producing dudes immediately. Perfect.

The area shown above is very simple. And more importantly player can only move within this small area so he has very limited options. What if we expand the area and add more spawners to challenge our AI count budget?

Spawn Type 2: Multiple Triggers and Spawners

In this sketch, player has the option to keep moving after he triggers the first two spawners. He can ended up with 5 AI spawned at the same time and it’s beyond our budget.

According to your game design, there are some fixes for this layout:

  • Make it impossible to survive too many AIs (not recommended, usually player outplays AI easily)
  • Maintain a list of AI spawned, always kills the oldest one in the list if a youngest one just spawned to make it over limit. (not perfect if player is free to look back and see AI despawn)
  • Run a Line of Sight detect, only despawn AIs when player not looking into that direction. If it’s a fixed camera angle game, only despawn when AIs are not in the camera view.

Our current project is a top-down shooter, so we can use distance detection to get rid of AIs out of camera view. But on the same token, top-down view means you can’t hide enemy spawning behind obstacles like FPS games usually do. I will discuss more problems and solutions in the 2nd part of this article.
